About

I’m a software engineer at the UK games company Improbable. I spend most of my time working on our backend infrastructure. My main focus is on monitoring performance and gameplay metrics, making heavy use of a Prometheus and Thanos.

I generally enjoy playing around with technology. Outside of my day job, I spend most of my computer time on machine learning. I also have a soft spot for implementing retro video games in OpenGL. I don’t have a favourite programming language, but if I did it would be Rust.

Before becoming a software engineer I worked as an academic mathematician. My research was in number theory, you can find some more details about what I worked on here.

When I’m not at a computer, I like to console myself by taking part in endurance sports. I haven’t decided if I’m a runner or a triathlete yet, but I definitely get a kick out of both. I’m a member of Wimbledon Windmilers and Clapham Chasers, competing for the former.

You can find me online at GitHub, LinkedIn, Strava, and some other places where I keep an even more reticent presence.
