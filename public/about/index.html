About

I’m a London-based software engineer at the UK games company Improbable. I spend most of my time working on our backend infrastructure. I’ve got a fair amount of experience in monitoring, particularly with Prometheus and Thanos. More recently, I’ve been working on automating scheduling and provisioning for on-prem machines hosting Kubernetes clusters.

I generally enjoy learning about and playing around with technology. I’m trying to blog regularly about academic papers. Code-wise, I’m spending a lot of time with OpenGL right now, but I haven’t decided if it’s a game or an engine yet.

Before becoming a software engineer I worked as an academic mathematician. My research was in number theory, you can find some more details about what I worked on here.

When I’m not at a computer, I like to console myself by taking part in endurance sports. My background is in running but I’m increasingly enjoying long-course triathlon. I’m a member of both Wimbledon Windmilers and Clapham Chasers. 我也花很多时间学习中文.

You can find me online at GitHub, LinkedIn, Strava, Goodreads and some other places where I keep an even more reticent presence.
