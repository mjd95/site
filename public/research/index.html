Research

Up until 2017, I worked as a research mathematician. I quit academia due to frustrations with the pace of university life, but I still love mathematics itself.

My area of interest was number theory, specifically automorphic form. Most of the things I worked on were inspired by either the Langlands program or Deligne’s conjecture. My work was a mixture of theory and computation.

My PhD was focused on Siegel modular forms. It included classical questions about Fourier coefficients and Hecke eigenvalues 1, 3, as well as questions about L-functions and statistics of Hecke eigenvalues 2.

After my PhD, I continued to work on Siegel modular forms, this time on special values of their L-functions 4, 6. I also worked on some computational aspects of classical modular forms and their local Galois representations 5, 7.

  • Fourier coefficients of degree two Siegel–Eisenstein series with trivial character at squarefree level
  • Local spectral equidistribution for Siegel modular forms with level
  • Hecke eigenvalues of Klingen–Eisenstein series of squarefee level
  • Explicit refinements of Boecherer’s conjecture for Siegel modular forms of squarefree level
  • Products of Eisenstein series and Fourier expansions of modular forms at cusps
  • Computing Petersson norms of Siegel cusp forms of degree two
  • Local epsilon factors and Galois representations of newforms
